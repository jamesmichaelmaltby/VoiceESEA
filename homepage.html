Our Mission

We want to eliminate racial discrimination against East and South East Asians (ESEA) by educating about, and amplifying voices of ESEA within the community. Our aim is for everyone to feel safe, respected and proud.


OUR ORIGINS

After witnessing the rise in hate crimes against ESEAs around the world as a result of COVID-19-fuelled prejudice, a group of friends decided to take a stand. Hence, Voice ESEA was born.

OUR PETITION

We launched a petition to the UK Government and Parliament. We gathered over 22,000 signatures and obtained a Government response.

The petition aimed to raise awareness of the rise in hate crimes against ESEAs, as well as push the Government in considering resourcing for victim support and non-profit groups campaigning against racism.

View our Reply

OUR POLICE DATA PROJECT

Voice ESEA’s Data Team worked with volunteers in identifying and distilling useful information to assist Voice ESEA with its aims of raising awareness and understanding the scope of anti-ESEA discrimination in the UK. As part of this, Voice ESEA issued Freedom of Information Act 2000 requests to over 40 police bodies across the UK to determine the rates of police-reported hate crime against ESEAs in 2019 and 2020 in different regions. ANIMOSITY AGAINST ESEAS

Since COVID-19, UK hate crimes against ESEAs rose significantly in some areas. We all suffer from the virus’ effects, but ESEA are also targeted by unprovoked verbal assaults, threats and violent attacks. Some examples:

  1. Student Jonathan Mok was beaten up in London in February 2020 (https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-london-54048546)
  2. Pensioner Mr Wang was permanently disabled and left with PTSD after a teenager attacked him, citing the virus and Mr Wang’s race (https://www.vice.com/en/article/4adzeb/anti-asian-hate-crimes-uk)
  3. Lecturer Dr Peng Wang attacked on a jog in Southampton in February 2021 (https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-hampshire-56209881)
  4. Chinese student attacked in Sheffield city centre in May with severe eye injuries in May 2021 (https://nextshark.com/sheffield-university-chinese-student-eye-injuries/)
  5. Yuanzhao Zhang, an engineer and former Cambridge University alumni was attacked in the local Co-op in September 2021 (https://www.cambridge-news.co.uk/news/cambridge-news/cambridgeshire-crime-brutal-racist-attack-21653532)
  6. Multiple accounts of NHS staff suffering from COVID-19-fuelled abuse (https://news.sky.com/story/covid-19-nhs-staff-fall-victim-to-anti-chinese-hate-crimes-amid-fears-violence-will-rise-when-lockdown-ends-12206686)

IPSO Mori conducted a survey in 2020 which found that 1 in 7 people in the UK intentionally avoided people of Chinese origin or appearance (https://www.ipsos.com/ipsos-mori/en-uk/covid-19-one-seven-people-would-avoid-people-chinese-origin-or-appearance).

YouGov survey of 5,000 people, commissioned by the Migration Observatory at the University of Oxford, investigated how the pandemic has shaped attitudes in relation to migrants and suggests that the British are most negative towards Chinese migrants (https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/711ba91c-f0a2-11eb-a2a3-afea84050239?shareToken=e8806c34d24c79f8c44e184e6d659616 / https://migrationobservatory.ox.ac.uk/press/new-study-finds-uk-residents-who-suffered-financially-in-the-pandemic-more-supportive-of-welfare-access-for-migrants-and-that-concern-about-chinese-immigration-is-high/).
